Tips & Tricks

Helpful advice for making the most of the competition and creating a strong submission.

Design

  • Prioritize clarity and readability—users may need to read text quickly during live conversation.
  • Design for different lighting conditions; the translation screen may be used indoors and outdoors.
  • Consider thumb-friendly touch targets, especially for one-handed use.
  • Ensure sufficient color contrast for users with varying levels of vision.

User Research

  • Talk to deaf or HoH individuals if possible—their lived experience will strengthen your design.
  • Document your research method and findings; judges want to see how insights shaped your choices.
  • Even simple methods (e.g. surveys, interviews, observation) can yield valuable insights.

Presentation

  • Practice with a timer; 7 minutes goes quickly.
  • Lead with your problem and solution, then walk through key screens.
  • Explain how your user research influenced at least one major design decision.
  • Prepare a brief overview of your team and design process at the start.
